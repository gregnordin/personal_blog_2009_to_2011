Moab 2011

Saturday, May 7, 2011

 

This year our Moab trip was somewhat abbreviated, consisting of 3.5 days of riding, from May 4 to May 7, instead of 6 days like last year due to some scheduling conflicts with work. We also had a shifting cast of characters. Mark & Pat from Huntsville, Alabama were not able to make it this year because Mark broke his leg a few months ago and isn’t quite ready to go yet. While they were sorely missed, we still had a fantastic time!


We stayed all 4 nights of our trip in Green River rather than Moab because it was more central to the trails we wanted to ride. While there is definitely a lot more happening in Moab than Green River, the convenience more than outweighed other considerations. Besides, Green River has one of our favorite dinner stops--Rays!


A new participant, Dave, had a helmet cam and made a video from his footage for each of the 4 days we rode. The videos are included below. If you can only watch one of them, be sure to look at Day 4 on which we rode the infamous 5 Miles of Hell trail, which was definitely a challenge!


This year I didn’t take as many pictures, but I’ve included links to some photos from Days 1 & 2. On Day 3 my camera began to die the same way all of my previous cameras have given up the ghost, i.e., grit in the lens mechanism which causes it to become stuck. Fortunately, it has since cleared itself. The camera has already lasted a lot longer (1.5 years) than my previous cameras (1 year at the most!) since I’ve been much more careful about protecting it from trail dust.


Below is a map with GPS tracks from each day’s ride. Day 2 (May 5) is broken up into two tracks.

Day 1 - Wednesday, May 4

Riding area: Chimney Rock

People: Matt, Dave, & Greg


Matt fortunately returned from California just in time to join us in another Moab adventure. He, Dave, & I loaded up my truck and Dave’s enclosed trailer and started off in the early afternoon. We stopped on our way to Green River to ride in the Chimney Rock area. I had been there once before while it was Dave & Matt’s first time. What a great place! There are some really technical trails as well as wide open trails so you get a fun mix of riding.

Photos from Day 1


After finishing our ride we went to Green River, checked into our hotel, and had a fine dinner at Rays.



Day 2 - Thursday, May 5

Riding area: White Wash, Dead Cow, Etc.

People: Matt, Dave, Greg, Steve, Mike, & Brian


Steve drove down early from Lehi and met us at 8:30 am at our hotel. The plan was to go out to White Wash, do a morning ride, and meet Mike & Brian who had to pack up Brian’s trailer from where they camped near Slickrock in Moab. After we got on the freeway and made it to our offramp (Ranch Rd), I realized I had not stopped for gas for the dirt bikes in Green River 20 miles behind us. So, we (Matt, Dave, & I) turned around, did the drive in reverse, got gas, & drove back to meet Steve at the White Wash trailhead. We had a fun morning ride exploring trails to the north of the trailhead that we had never been on before. The single track was a lot of fun, and we went on parts of the Orange Trail which is more for ATVs & UTVs.


After meeting Mike & Brian, we started out on the main ride for the day which involved going on White Wash, down Red Canyon & Dead Cow, checking out Hobbit Town, and then up the Tube, at the head of which is the infamous 3 miles of whoops. Rather than doing all of the whoops (which have been somewhat knocked down from when we rode them last year), we turned onto Tenmile Point Road to the slickrock trails. We went down Red Slot and then made our way over to Mary’s Trail, which was spectacular! We then went to some other trails (don’t know their names) that were likewise amazing, and returned to the trucks. Along the way, Matt & I lost Dave (the other guys had already taken a different way back) and we spent quite a bit of time looking for him. He was also looking for us and we eventually met back up at the trucks. It was another fantastic day of riding!

Photos from Day 2


After we got loaded up and were just starting to drive out of the trailhead I got a phone call from Aaron. He had planned to ride his street bike from Provo to Green River after he finished his last class for the day at 4:00 pm. He was calling to say that he had arrived at our hotel. We swung by there and picked him up and went to Ray’s for another good dinner before calling it a day. Steve then drove back to Lehi since he had other obligations. Mike & Brian went to Temple Mountain where they set up camp to continue with their ride itinerary. For the next 2 days Aaron, Matt, Dave, and I were on our own.



Day 3 - Friday, May 6

Riding area: Loop up Kane Creek and down Pritchett Canyon

People: Aaron, Matt, Dave, & Greg


After breakfast at the hotel we drove to the Yamaha shop in Moab to see if we could get parts to fix Dave’s rear brake. On the first day of the trip a rock hit his brake line causing it to leak. Result: no rear brake. Since we planned to come down Pritchett Canyon, we figured we’d better do something about  it. Unfortunately the shop did not have a replacement brake line in stock. So, we went for the McGyver fix: a small hose clamp torqued around a rubber tire patch to seal the leak. Fortunately, it worked great!

Day 4 - Saturday, May 7

Riding area: 5 Miles of Hell

People: Aaron, Matt, Dave, & Greg


For the final day we saved the best for last: the 5 Miles of Hell (5MOH) trail! None of us had done it before. Mike & Brian did it last year and didn’t want to do it again. We tried to find someone who was experienced with the trail to act as guide, but were not successful. We figured that with a map, gps, and some knowledge from online descriptions, we would just go for it. Wow, what a day it turned out to be!


5MOH is actually 8 miles long, and goes through extremely rough country. The thing that makes the trail so challenging is that it has one tough obstacle after another--they just don’t stop! Many of the obstacles are difficult but manageable. However, there were one or two that were especially tough, the worst of which is called the Crux.

It took us 6 hrs 40 minutes to complete the ride, which was a 21 mile loop including 5MOH. The actual 5MOH trail took the vast majority of the time (~6 hrs). We were exhausted by the end, but it was nonetheless a great day! In fact, I can’t wait to do this trail again!
